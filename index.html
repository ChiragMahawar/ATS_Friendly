ATS Best Practices
& Pro Tips

Everything you need to know to beat Applicant Tracking Systems and land more interviews.

01

Use Standard Section Headings

Stick to recognizable headings like "Work Experience", "Education", "Skills", and "Projects". Creative headings like "My Journey" confuse parsers.

02

Avoid Tables, Columns & Graphics

Most ATS cannot parse multi-column layouts, tables, images, or text boxes. Use a simple, single-column format with clear hierarchy.

03

Mirror Job Description Keywords

Copy relevant keywords and phrases directly from the job posting. ATS systems rank resumes by keyword match percentage.

04

Use a Standard File Format

Submit your resume as a .docx or .pdf file. Avoid .jpg, .png, or complex PDF designs with embedded fonts.

05

Include a Professional Summary

A 2-3 sentence summary at the top helps ATS identify your profile category and seniority level quickly.

06

Quantify Your Achievements

Instead of "improved performance", write "improved page load speed by 40%, increasing user retention by 15%".

07

Spell Out Acronyms

Write "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)" the first time. Some ATS search for the full term, others for the acronym.

08

Use Simple Bullet Points

Stick to standard bullet characters (•, -, *). Fancy icons or custom symbols get stripped or garbled by parsers.
